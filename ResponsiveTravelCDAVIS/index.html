Belo Horizonte

"Beautiful Horizon" is a busy city surrounded by natural beauty. As an important international financial and business centre, Belo Horizonte is a cosmopolitan city, with a wealth of stylish delights from first-class restaurants to high-end shopping. But it is also a city with deep historical roots and a strong identity of its own.

The city features a mixture of contemporary adn classical buildings, and is home to several modern Brazilian architectural icons, such as the Pmapulha Complex. With urban planning inspired by Washington, D.C. the city has employed notable programs in urban revitalization and food security, for which it has been awareded international accolades.

The city is built on several hills and is completely surrounded by mountains. There are several large parks in the immediate surroundings of Belo Horizonte such as The Mangabeiras Park (Parque das Mangabeiras) in the southeast hills of Curral Ridge (Serra do Curral), and The Jambeiro Woods (Malta do Jambeiro) nature reserve with over a hundred species of birds and ten species of mammal.

The city is known to be one of the host cities of the 1950 and 2014 FIFA world Cup, also sharing as host of the 2013 FIFA Confederations Cup and the football tournament durring the 2016 Summer Olympics.

Belo Horizonte has a vibrant nightlife and arts scene, some excellent museums, a cosmopolitan buzz and a pleasant climate. Moreover, it is centrally located and therefore a convenient springboard to a few the most picturesque historical colonial towns in Brazil, like Ouro Preto.
